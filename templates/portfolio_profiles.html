Portfolio Profiles and Manager Workflow

This page breaks down each portfolio profile and shows the type of review a portfolio manager would complete before making an investment decision. The goal is to assess suitability, mandate fit, risk tolerance, constraints, and implementation priorities before adding any new security to the portfolio.

Portfolio Manager Workflow

  1. Identify the client or entity and confirm legal ownership of the account.
  2. Review compliance status, including KYC, AML, source of wealth, and tax reporting status.
  3. Confirm investment objectives, benchmark, and expected return range.
  4. Assess risk tolerance, risk capacity, and maximum acceptable drawdown.
  5. Review financial position, liquidity needs, liabilities, and cash flow profile.
  6. Check all portfolio constraints, including legal, ESG, currency, and concentration limits.
  7. Review behavioural factors that may affect implementation or reporting style.
  8. Confirm mandate type, rebalancing frequency, and IPS requirements.
  9. Decide whether a proposed investment is suitable for the mandate.
  10. Determine implementation size, monitoring frequency, and reporting expectations.

Portfolio Manager Checklist

  • Client identity or legal entity confirmed
  • KYC and AML status complete
  • Source of wealth and source of funds understood
  • Investment objective clearly defined
  • Benchmark identified
  • Risk tolerance consistent with proposed assets
  • Liquidity requirements reviewed
  • Legal and regulatory constraints checked
  • ESG restrictions or preferences checked
  • Currency rules checked
  • Maximum position size checked
  • Behavioural risks considered
  • Mandate and IPS requirements respected
  • Monitoring and reporting plan appropriate
