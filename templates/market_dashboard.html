Market Dashboard

What this dashboard does

This page helps a portfolio manager screen securities before moving to detailed analysis. It is designed to reduce the list quickly by applying risk, return, valuation, and conviction filters.

What each smart preset means

Low Risk: highlights more stable securities with lower market sensitivity. Typically includes assets with beta at or below 1 and moderate volatility. These are commonly used for capital preservation, income generation, or defensive positioning within a portfolio.

High Conviction (6% position): identifies the strongest investment ideas based on expected return, volatility, and risk-adjusted performance. These are high-confidence opportunities where the manager allocates more capital, similar to concentrated or high-conviction strategies.

Core Holdings (3.5% position): represents stable, well-balanced investments that form the foundation of the portfolio. These provide diversification and consistent exposure while still contributing to overall returns.

Satellite / Diversifiers (3% position): includes supporting positions used to enhance diversification or gain exposure to specific sectors, themes, or regions. These positions are smaller because they carry either more uncertainty or a narrower role.

Exploratory / High Risk (1–2% position): captures higher-risk or less certain opportunities. These are included in small sizes to allow upside participation while limiting downside impact.

Income / Defensive: focuses on securities that provide stability or income, such as bond ETFs, dividend-paying equities, and defensive sectors (e.g. utilities, healthcare, consumer staples). These are typically used in lower-risk portfolios or to reduce overall volatility.

Best Ranked: highlights securities that combine strong overall scores with acceptable risk-adjusted returns. In this project, that means a score of at least 5 and a Sharpe-like ratio above 0.3, making them suitable candidates for inclusion in most portfolios.

Cash (5% allocation): used when no suitable investment meets the required criteria or to manage overall portfolio risk. Holding cash is an active decision and serves as a control mechanism during uncertain conditions.
